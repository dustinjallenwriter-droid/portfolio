DICE.fm Style Guide (Editing, Writing, Internal Database Creation)

For this assignment, I collaborated with a partner to identify a brand with a recognizable style, but no publicly available style guide. We chose DICE.fm, a Ticketmaster competitor with highly stylized documentation but no clear internal guidelines for writers.

To suit the brand's digital identity, we decided to move away from the PDF output and exclusively present the style guide as a knowledge base using Notion. (Link)

Skills

  • Editorial standards & voice/tone definition
  • Content audit & taxonomy from live docs
  • Knowledge base structuring in Notion
  • Collaboration and communication

Challenges

  • Reverse-engineering a style system without an official guide
  • Designing for collaborative, living documentation (Notion)
  • Striking a balance between brand identity and technical writing best practices

Jimmy's Site (UX Design, Web Design, Prototyping)

This was a multi-month collaboration in which we moved through many stages of user research and development to create a fully functional help center for a brand that had no online help content whatsoever.

To develop this site, we used UX processes like:

  • User personas
  • User journeys
  • Pathway tracking
  • Card-sorting (three rounds)
  • In-lab testing (two rounds)

The project was highly collaborative, but I took the lead on authoring and designing our HTML, CSS, and JavaScript..

Skills

  • UX research (cardsorts, usability tests, pathway tracking)
  • Figma prototype → web design handoff (HTML/CSS/JS)
  • Microcopy & interaction design
  • Iterative development
  • Mobile-first & responsive design

Challenges

  • Translating evolving prototypes into production-ready code
  • Learning to optimize UX research methods on the fly
  • Making collaborative decisions on conflicting testing results

Knight vs Pierce (Educational Content Development, Research)

Of the five educational programs I wrote and developed for Barkerville, the two-man reenactment of "The Trial of James Barry for the Murder of Charles Morgan Blessing" was the most significant. This dramatization of real court reporting balanced authenticity with humor to engage audiences of all ages and language backgrounds.

In its inaugural year, the CEO granted us the "Audience Choice Award" for most frequently praised program in vistor feedback forms (2015). It ran until 2020 with minor revisions along the way based on audience and stakeholder feedback.

Skills

  • Archival research
  • Adaptating and re-purposing documentation
  • Diverse audience engagement
  • Creative writing

Challenges

  • Balancing historical authenticity with accessibility and humor
  • Fact-checking and verifying antiquated sources
  • Adapting material over multiple seasons based on conflicting feedback from curatorial management, entertainment management, and audiences

Adult Literacy Volunteering (Document Design, Editing)

As an Adult Literacy Volunteer at West Neighbourhood House, I customize my tutoring to the individual needs and goals of each learner.

One learner's goal was to publish a book. He's quite skilled at reading, but has significant mental blocks when trying to type or write for himself. Using a dictation method, we completed his book-length religious memoir.

We're nearly done designing the document in FrameMaker. I do the technical while work and guide him through the process of creating character, paragraph, master page styles to suit his preferences. We collaboratively used AI to generate the book's images.

.pdf

Skills

  • Accessible document design (FrameMaker)
  • Co-authoring via dictation
  • Editorial coaching & structural editing

Challenges

  • Maintaining learner’s voice while editing for clarity
  • Designing an approachable, confidence-building process
  • Lending technical skills to learner's creative vision
